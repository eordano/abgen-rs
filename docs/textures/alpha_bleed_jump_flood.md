# Alpha-bleed is a jump-flood nearest-seed fill, not an averaging dilation

**Why it matters:** Standalone textures with alpha were the single biggest byte-parity residual: most of their BC7 blocks differed from Unity even after mode selection and encoder parity work, because the *input pixels* differed. Unity's `alphaIsTransparency` preprocessing rewrites the RGB of transparent pixels before compression, and abgen-rs's previous reconstruction of that pass (an iterative 4-connected neighbor-averaging dilation) produced the right reach but the wrong values almost everywhere it acted. Once the input RGB of a transparent region is wrong, every BC7 block touching it diverges, no matter how faithful the encoder is.

**How it was recovered:** The pass was reverse-engineered black-box, without Unity source. A synthetic probe PNG — isolated opaque seed pixels with unique loud colors, two-seed contests, an alpha staircase, and a hard color-banded edge — was substituted for a real wearable's `image.png` and run through the actual reference converter (Unity 6000.2.6f2 batchmode, the same binary that produced the parity corpus). Decoding the emitted BC7 bundle exposes the dilation field exactly: a single seed expands into a fractal lattice with holes at the diagonal neighbors, two seeds carve nearest-wins territories with no blended values anywhere, partial-alpha pixels act as seeds, alpha is never modified, and the fill stops 31 pixels out. The lattice holes are the giveaway: a pixel is reached only if its offset from a seed decomposes into *distinct* powers of two per axis.

**The algorithm:** five plus-shaped passes (no diagonals) at descending offsets 16, 8, 4, 2, 1. Each pass, every transparent pixel inspects the four pixels at ±offset on each axis in the previous pass's state, and adopts a candidate's nearest-seed when that seed is strictly closer to it in L1 distance than its current best; pixels already filled keep improving on later passes. After the final pass each reached pixel copies its seed's RGB byte-for-byte. This is the classic GPU jump-flood approximation of a Voronoi fill, restricted to axis taps. The descending schedule and the keep-improving rule are both load-bearing: ascending offsets or freeze-once-filled produce visibly different territory boundaries that the reference contradicts.

`src/alpha_bleed.rs` implements exactly this and nothing else — no averaging, no rounding rule, no pass-count tuning. The rewritten pass reproduces the overwhelming majority of mip-0 BC7 blocks of power-of-two PNG-with-alpha reference textures byte-for-byte. The known residuals it does not address: non-power-of-two sources (the divergence there is in the resize filter, upstream of the bleed), JPEG sources (decoder mismatch, also upstream), and a small within-reach tie-break tail characterised below.

The in-glb texture path stays unbled, as before: `CustomGltfImporter` does not enable `alphaIsTransparency`, and bleeding those textures regresses parity.

## What the residual actually is (investigation, not bleed-fixable)

A full split of the same-dims RGBA-PNG BC7 population isolated the textures whose *opaque* region is byte-perfect but whose *transparent* region still diverges from Unity (363 such "pure-bleed" standalone textures on val300-windows). Three candidate causes were tested and the first two ruled out:

- **The 31-pixel reach cap is correct.** Decoding the reference's transparent texels and measuring their L1 distance to the nearest seed, genuine value disagreements are spread across distances 1–31 and essentially vanish beyond 31 (a handful of BFS off-by-one at exactly 32). Far-field transparent texels keep their *source* RGB (commonly solid white), which abgen already reproduces — Unity does not fill them. (Two coordinate traps make this easy to misread: the stored mip-0 payload is vertically flipped relative to the source, so a source texel `(x,y)` lands at payload `(x, h-1-y)`; and BC7 quantises edge-texel alpha, so the *decoded* alpha mask is not the bleed seed mask — always seed from the **source** alpha, not the decoded reference alpha. Measuring against the decoded mask manufactures thousands of phantom "wrong-seed" texels that are really opaque edge encoding.)
- **The bleed runs once at mip-0, then box-filter — no per-mip re-bleed.** Per-mip-level transparent wrong-rate is flat across the chain (~0.02 % at mip-0 rising only to ~0.09 % at the deepest non-trivial level), exactly what one expects from a single mip-0 bleed propagated downward; a per-mip re-dilation would blow up the deep-mip rate, which it does not.
- **The remaining disagreements are a jump-flood tie-break detail, ~0.02 % of transparent texels.** Where two seeds sit at equal minimum L1 distance, abgen's plus-tap JFA and Unity occasionally adopt different seeds (e.g. a clean L1-21 tie picking `(24,108)` vs `(28,104)`). This is the classic JFA-vs-exact-Voronoi boundary ambiguity. Several alternative rules were tried — last-tap-wins (`<=`), U/D-first and D/U-first tap orders, an extra trailing offset-1 pass, two full sweeps, an all-offset-1 sweep, full-descending `128…1`, and exact bounded L1 Voronoi with smallest/largest-index tie-breaks — and **none** flips a single texture to byte-identical or even reduces the byte difference of any texture (`examples/bleedvariant.rs`).

**Why no bleed change is worth landing:** an oracle that substitutes the reference's own decoded transparent RGB (a *perfect* bleed, modulo BC7 lossiness) before re-encoding flips only 1 of 363 textures to byte-identical and reduces the diff on 6 (`examples/oraclebatch.rs`). The differing blocks are non-flat transparent regions where bled-colour gradients meet, and they carry the same AVX2-vs-ISPC BC7 float-order divergence that blocks the opaque population. The bleed is therefore **not** the parity bottleneck for this population — the BC7 encoder is — so the rule above is left unchanged.

Analysis tooling: `examples/bleedsplit.rs` (alpha-keyed transparent-vs-opaque diff split + verdict tally), `examples/bleedtrue.rs` (source-alpha-masked genuine-bleed-vs-edge-encoder split — the seed-mask trap), `examples/bleedreal.rs` (`--mips` per-level wrong-rate), `examples/bleedvariant.rs` (re-encode under candidate bleed rules, byte-compare to reference), `examples/oraclebatch.rs` (perfect-bleed byte-id ceiling), `examples/bleedloc.rs` (per-texel deviation geometry).
