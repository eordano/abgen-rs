# BC7 mode-3 and mode-7: partition scoring, endpoint rounding, and the mode-7 floor

**Why it matters:** Modes 3 and 7 are the 2-subset opaque and 2-subset RGBA BC7 modes. Both diverged from the reference in two recurring ways: the wrong partition was front-loaded in the shortlist (so the reference's chosen partition was never even evaluated), and the per-channel endpoints landed off by one in raw quantized space. Mode 7 additionally diverged badly on its alpha endpoints. Each of these flips the encoded bytes even when the decoded image is close, blocking byte-identical output.

**How it works:** The durable insights are these. First, partition shortlisting should score candidates by within-subset variance reduction (the same objective the inner endpoint fit optimizes), not by inter-subset mean separation — aligning the shortlist metric with the refinement objective puts the reference's chosen partition near the top far more often. For mode 7, alpha is the dominant partition signal: scoring partitions by alpha alone outperforms RGB-only scoring substantially, because mode 7 concentrates on alpha-bearing blocks. Second, almost all surviving endpoint differences in mode 3 are off-by-one in raw quantized space, and the pbit choice is locked to the rounding direction of the channel quantizer — pbit and rounding form one coupled decision and cannot be searched independently. The pbit flip rate is near 50% across slots, so there is no globally preferred pbit; it is data-driven per block. These properties hold across the 2-subset modes (and mode 1, which shares the estimator).

The mode-7 alpha endpoints are a documented hard floor and a partly negative finding. Mode 7 fires on many alpha-flat blocks, where the high alpha endpoint does not affect the decoded (constant) alpha but its bit pattern still costs a full block mismatch. The low alpha endpoint follows a clean rounding rule, but the high alpha endpoint is not a function of source alpha at all: in flat-alpha subsets the reference's high-minus-low alpha spans a wide range with no extractable pattern. Replaying the fit with the reference's exact selectors reproduces the low endpoint but not the high one, because the residual is float-precision noise in bc7e's least-squares solve that cannot be matched without bit-exact float replay. Several snap and zero-contribution variants were tried and moved nothing. The conclusion is that the mode-7 match rate is capped without exact bc7e float reproduction; the only structural alternative is to route alpha-flat 2-subset RGBA blocks to mode 1 or mode 3 instead of mode 7 by penalizing mode 7 when alpha is constant.
